How I Stay Consistent While Learning to Code

One of the hardest parts of learning to code isn’t understanding concepts—it’s staying consistent. There were days I felt motivated and days I didn’t want to open my laptop at all.

At first, I tried studying for long hours, which quickly led to burnout. Later, I switched to shorter, focused sessions. Even 30 minutes a day made a big difference. I also stopped comparing myself to others. Seeing people build amazing projects used to discourage me. Instead, I started comparing myself to my past progress. Another thing that helped was building small projects. Completing something—even tiny—gave me confidence and motivation to continue.
Consistency isn’t about being perfect. It’s about showing up, even on days when you don’t feel like it.
If you keep going one step at a time, progress will come.
