Beginner Mistakes I Made While Learning JavaScript

JavaScript was the most challenging part of my learning journey. Not because it was impossible—but because I approached it the wrong way at first.

One major mistake I made was trying to memorize syntax instead of understanding concepts. I believed good developers remembered everything. Later, I realized even experienced developers Google things every day.
Another mistake was ignoring error messages. I used to panic whenever I saw red text in the console. Over time, I learned that errors are actually helpful—they tell you exactly what went wrong.
I also spent too much time watching tutorials without building anything on my own. Real learning only started when I began making small projects and breaking things myself.
The biggest lesson I learned is this:
Feeling stuck means you’re growing.
JavaScript is hard—but it gets easier when you stop being afraid of mistakes.
